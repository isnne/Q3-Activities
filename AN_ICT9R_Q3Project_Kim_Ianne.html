10 ways to avoid fire accidents



Unplug Items You’re Not Using

Excess electricity flowing through your home's items is always a possible fire threat. Computers, televisions, game systems, and other electronic devices utilize power even when they are turned off. That implies they can always encounter a spike or simply overheat and catch fire.


The constant flow of electricity into them also serves as a fuel source for a fire. You may reduce your odds of a fire by disconnecting these gadgets while you're not using them and hence not paying attention to them.




Never Leave Flames Unattended

If you're cooking or using any other form of flame or high heat, you should keep an eye on it at all times. Once you start preparing anything, you never want to leave the stove.


By keeping a watch on these sorts of flames, you may easily determine whether there is a problem. This then means you'd be able to address the issue swiftly and ensure the safety of you and your family.




Turn Off Heated Appliances When You Leave the House

Your dryer generates a significant amount of heat, as does your dishwasher. These are all items you don't want to leave running when you're not at home as they could easily catch fire.





Don’t Smoke In the Home

If you do smoke, it is critical that you do it in a well-ventilated environment and that you totally extinguish the cigarette before disposing it. Cigarettes include a variety of compounds and ingredients that can continue to burn even after you believe you've extinguished them.


This flame can then catch fire inside your bin or even outside your home. You may reduce this danger by smoking outside, keeping an eye out for stray ashes, and completely extinguishing the cigarette before putting it away.




Use Surge Protectors

Make sure that all of your electronics are hooked into surge protectors, at the absolute least. The most likely time for an electrical fire is during a surge in power, therefore by putting goods into a surge protector, you can stop worrying about the extra voltage getting to the object and starting a fire.


The surge protector prevents that excess from entering and can significantly lower your chance of starting a fire or its intensity if you do.
